Anxiety Disorders

Anxiety disorders involve more than temporary worry or fear. For people with anxiety disorders, symptoms can grow and interfere with daily life.

Common Symptoms

  • Excessive worry that's difficult to control
  • Restlessness, feeling on edge
  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Sleep disturbances
  • Physical symptoms: rapid heartbeat, sweating, trembling

Treatment Options

Effective approaches include cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT), medication (such as SSRIs), relaxation and mindfulness practices, and lifestyle changes.

Depression

Major depressive disorder is a common and serious condition that negatively affects how a person feels, thinks, and acts. It can cause persistent sadness and loss of interest in activities.

Common Symptoms

  • Persistent sad, anxious, or "empty" mood
  • Loss of interest in activities
  • Changes in appetite or weight
  • Sleep problems (insomnia or oversleeping)
  • Fatigue or loss of energy, thoughts of death or suicide

Treatment Options

Common treatments include psychotherapy (CBT, interpersonal therapy), antidepressant medications, lifestyle interventions, and, in certain cases, other medical treatments under professional supervision.

Find Help for Depression

Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)

PTSD can develop after exposure to traumatic events. Symptoms may include intrusive memories, nightmares, avoidance, negative mood, and hyperarousal.

Common Symptoms

  • Intrusive memories or flashbacks
  • Avoiding reminders of the event
  • Negative changes in mood and thinking
  • Heightened startle response or hypervigilance

Treatment Options

Trauma-focused therapies (e.g., prolonged exposure, CPT), EMDR, medication, and supportive group or individual therapy are common evidence-based options.

Find Help for PTSD

Bipolar Disorder

Bipolar disorder causes significant shifts in mood, energy, activity levels, and the ability to carry out day-to-day tasks. These shifts include manic or hypomanic episodes and depressive episodes.

Common Symptoms

  • Periods of unusually elevated mood (mania or hypomania)
  • Periods of depression with low energy and hopelessness
  • Impulsive or risky behaviors during manic phases

Treatment Options

Mood stabilizers, psychotherapy, psychoeducation, and close medical monitoring form the core of evidence-based management for bipolar disorder.

Find Help for Bipolar Disorder

Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD)

OCD involves unwanted intrusive thoughts (obsessions) and repetitive behaviors (compulsions) performed to reduce distress. These behaviors can be time-consuming and disruptive.

Common Symptoms

  • Persistent intrusive thoughts or worries
  • Repetitive rituals (handwashing, checking)
  • Distress and interference with daily life

Treatment Options

Exposure and response prevention (ERP), a form of CBT, is the gold-standard therapy. Medication (SSRIs) can also help when combined with therapy.

Find Help for OCD

Schizophrenia

Schizophrenia affects how a person thinks, feels, and behaves. Symptoms can include hallucinations, delusions, disorganized speech, and social withdrawal.

Common Symptoms

  • Hallucinations (hearing or seeing things that aren't there)
  • Delusions (fixed false beliefs)
  • Disorganized thinking or speech

Treatment Options

Antipsychotic medications, psychosocial support, coordinated specialty care, and family education are commonly used to manage schizophrenia.
